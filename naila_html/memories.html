Various Memories


♪ We don't remember days, we remember moments.-Cesare Pavese



Living in US

In the summer of my 4th grade, my family moved to the US(it was a last-minute decision). My father returned to India soon after to manage his business while the rest of my family lived with my aunt at her house. That summer was one of the best ones as my cousins(all three) had summer vacation and as I would join school which would start in September, we would have three whole months for fun. My cousin-who is three years older than me- and I would go to the public library every day as they would set up various activities. There was this one contest where we would have to read a book and write its summary. On submitting the summary we would get various prizes such as free meals in various different resteraunts, goodies,etc. Since this contest was only for children below 13, my brothers,my cousin and I would save some coupons for my older cousins and we would go to the resteraunts. I became especially close to my cousins as I was just used to seeing them once a year and since we now had more time to spend together we would do various activites such as making necklaces out of beads, giving each other makeovers, imitating the people we knew and watching TV and movies together. Within a month, my family started renting an apartment which was just two miles away from my cousins' house. I started to go to Swanson Elementary School along with my brothers and younger cousin. School was fun as there would be several events throughout the year. There was this one event where all students of non-American descent had to bring food which is a part of their culture- so that day we had a chance to try out different cuisines such as Greek,Spanish,Indian,Italian,etc.

Middle School

The next year we had to go to Wisconsin Hills Middle School and this was the phase where we became a bit more responsible as we were handed out assignments and tests were conducted. This school too was a lot of fun as we would have several programmes each month. There was this one event for charity which would occur around the time of Thanksgiving. We were given three days to donate canned food and if the entire school (grades 6,7 and 8) managed to donate more than a 1000 items, the principal would dress up as a turkey. There was another event where each child who had donated 10 items or more was allowed to throw a pie at any of the teachers who had agreed to participate in this. This event occured during lunch and a row of teachers would sit on chairs and the entire grade would cheer those who were throwing pies at the teachers. We did not have much pressure of studies and I had a lot of fun as several of my friends lived in my neighbourhood-before most of them moved away while I was in 7th.

Returning to India

In the summer of 7th grade, my family decided to return to India. This was as it was becoming increasingly hard for my dad to manage his business back here as well as to be with us. When we returned, we lived in a hotel for a few days as the finishing touches were being added to my new house. My parents encouraged me to join Vidyaranya as they liked the fact that it did not put too much pressure on students. Initially, I was against this but later relented. My first day of Vidyaranya was memorable in a way. I was put in one of the two sections and the teacher had noticed me but did not ask me my name until the end of the class. I talked to a few of the students but during English class, the teacher decided that I should be put in the other section so as to balance out the number of students. She took me to the other section and that's where I met most of my friends. School was fun in Vidyaranya. We also had excursions to places like Kaigal, Bijapur, Hampi, etc. Excursions were especially fun as we would shop together and tour the places. We would go sightseeing in urban areas and help out with farming and other activities in rural areas.

Junior and Senior Years

For 11th and 12th grade, I went to Meridian School. I did not like the school as much as I felt that they would waste a lot of time and then call us for classes on Saturday. There were several days where we would only have a class or two a day as there was one event or another which was approaching. As studies were taken more seriously now, the only time my friends and I could actually enjoy was during lunch. However, since we had free periods before annual days, sports days,etc we would utilise that time to study together or chat.

Summer

Right after board exams, I started preparing for JEE and the other competitive exams. As I was not serious about them and since my Chemistry teachers were switched at the last moment, I did not fare well in most of the exams. However, during June, I got a lot of free time to do the things I like-especially reading. Since we had exams frequently, I would not get enough time to read at all during the school days. I could only manage to read a book or two in the holidays. One of my friends who had not written any competitive exams spent most of her vacation reading novels and she recommended some of them to me. I also had time to catch up with old friends and shop a bit. In July, I had to decide which college I would be going to. I had already received admission in Jawaharlal Nehru Trust University(JNTU) as well as Osmania College but I had my hopes pinned on IIIT-H. My father preferred me to write the exams again next year if I would not receive admission here. However, I got admitted to this college in the ECE stream. Since the campus is only 45 minutes away from my house (in traffic), I usually go back home for the weekend and the holidays.
